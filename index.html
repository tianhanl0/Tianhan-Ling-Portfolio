About Me

My name is Tianhan Ling, a graduate student from Carnegie Mellon University in the program of data analytics for science. I finished my undergraduate in University of California, Irvine with a major in Mathematics and minor Statistics which gives me a proficient background in linear algebra and probability toward further learning of data science. During the study at CMU, I got a brief understanding of exploratory data analysis, different machine learning models, ways of visualizing data and dealing with large-scaled datasets.

I had worked in both insurance and advertising companies, which gave me the abilities of quickly integrating into the team as well as some collaboration experience. I have helped to establish the Publicis Groupe's internal database on advertising price with my colleagues. Through collaboration with different advertising teams, I got to know the data format of different advertisements and points I needed to pay attention to. And finally completed the sorting of 10k+ data within three days.

In university, the programming languages I used the most are Python and R. For both of my internships I used Excel. And beside those, I also self-studied SQL and Tableau and finished a visualization project based on those two techniques with the topic of covid death and vaccination rate. And I hope those experiences and skills can help me with my future career.
